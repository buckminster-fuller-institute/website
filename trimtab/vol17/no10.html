BFI Updates

The Rainforest Solutions Project Wins the 2016 Fuller Challenge

Congratulations to the Winner of the 2016 Fuller Challenge, The Rainforest Solutions Project: a collaborative effort between three environmental organizations, which played a critical role in developing one of the most extraordinary approaches to conservation, social justice, and indigenous rights in recent memory. The full announcement as well as a statement from the Review Committee can be found here.
2016 Fuller Challenge Conferring Ceremony

Join BFI at the 2016 Fuller Challenge Conferring Ceremony in New York City! We will celebrate the work of the 2016 Fuller Challenge winner and finalists at the Wythe Hotel. The evening will entail a short program about the Fuller Challenge, the awarding of the Omnioculi prize and $100,000 check to the winner of the Challenge, and celebratory drinks. Event details and a link to register can be found here.
Volunteer with BFI!

BFI is seeking volunteers to help with the conferring ceremony on November 16th, as well as longer-term volunteers to help with tasks at the institute during the months of November and December. Click for more information on BFI's current volunteer needs.
Support our work

Please consider a tax-deductible donation of any amount to support BFI as we seek to continue and expand educational programming, including the Fuller Challenge, in 2017. BFI is a small, non-profit organization that survives in large part thanks to the generosity of its individual donors and supporters. Film clip on 2016 Fuller Challenge Winner

Growing out of three decades of work by the Prince of Wales to combat climate change and find innovative solutions to the global environmental crisis, the 2010 film Harmony is an urgent, accessible, and practical call to action. This clip from the film provides an elegant summary of the accomplishment of the 2016 Fuller Challenge Winner, the Rainforest Solutions Project.

Limited Edition Portfolio available on BFI's online store

A Limited Edition print portfolio of new works was created to celebrate the 120th anniversary of the birth of Buckminster Fuller.
