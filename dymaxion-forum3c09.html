Dymaxion Forum

Dymaxion Forum

The Dymaxion Forum is dedicated to gathering the most interesting, up-to-date information, viewpoints, and observations advancing the legacy of Buckminster Fuller on Spaceship Earth today and into the future.

Welcoming Our 2016 Program Fellows
Wednesday, 27 April 2016

Each year, BFI welcomes two outstanding students or early-career practitioners to our Fellowship Program. The Fellowship provides a unique opportunity to contextualize systems thinking through immersion in the Fuller Challenge review cycle. We are thrilled to announce our 2016 Fellows, Christina Ciardullo and Devon Artis-White. Click to read more about their experience with BFI.

Laka Competition '16: Architecture that Reacts
Monday, 25 April 2016

Laka invites architects, students, engineers, biologists, sociologists, designers, thinkers and organizations to submit their ideas for innovative architecture that adjusts dynamically to current needs and circumstances. Regular registration (75 USD) is open until October 1st. The final submission deadline is November 1st, 2016. More information can be found here.

Operating Manual: The Biggest Little Book I Know
Monday, 25 April 2016

The Week magazine recently published a list of media theorist Douglas Rushkoff's "6 favorite books about big ideas" - and Fuller's Operating Manual for Spaceship Earth was among them. Click to see the full list and to purchase the Operating Manual e-book.

2010 Fuller Challenge Winner in Sianyanga, Zimbabwe
Monday, 25 April 2016

A recent Pacific Standard article explores the application of 2010 Fuller Challenge Winner Allan Savory's Holistic Planned Grazing Model, detailing its comprehensive benefits to communities and ecosystems in Zimbabwe. Click to read more.

Living Future Conference '16
Monday, 25 April 2016

Celebrate a decade of innovation at ‪the Living Future Conference '16, "Truth + Transparency: Celebrating a Decade of Innovation" on May 11-13th in Seattle, WA. Living Future is the forum for leading minds in the green building movement seeking solutions to the most daunting global issues of our time. BFI's network has been offered a 10% conference discount. Click for details.

"The Strange Brilliance of Buckminster Fuller" on WNPR
Monday, 25 April 2016

A recent WNPR interview explored Fuller's contributions to society in dialogue with three admirers who are deeply familiar with his work: Bonnie Devarco, Jonathan Keats, and Kurt Przybilla. The conversation covers synergy, the Dymaxion car, Black Mountain College, the geodesic dome, and more. Click to listen to the interview.

Biomimicry Food Systems Challenge Deadline
Thursday, 21 April 2016

Look to the abundance of lessons nature has to offer and develop a biomimetic design that solves an important food system challenge while supporting the health of our planet.

The Biomimicry Institute is accepting submissions for the Food Systems Challenge until May 11th, 2016.

New Design from 2013 Fuller Challenge Semi-finalist Loowatt
Tuesday, 22 March 2016

Loowatt was a 2013 Fuller Challenge Semi-finalist.

Allegra Fuller Snyder's 2015 Conferring Ceremony Speech Now Online
Tuesday, 22 March 2016

At the 2015 Fuller Challenge Conferring Ceremony in Brooklyn, New York, Buckminster Fuller's daughter Allegra Fuller Snyder spoke about her father's influence. She states: "It was also like having a magician for a father - with these wonderful, unexpected surprises that he would pull forth from the hat that was his mind."

Bucky Inc.: Architecture in the Age of Radio by Mark Wigley
Tuesday, 22 March 2016

Released in December 2015, Bucky Inc. offers a deep exploration of Richard Buckminster Fuller's work and thought to shed new light on the questions raised by our increasingly electronic world. It shows that Fuller's entire career was a multi-dimensional reflection on the architecture of radio. He always insisted that the real site of architecture is the electromagnetic spectrum.

New Book: Last Futures by Douglas Murphy

Last Futures: Nature, Technology, and the End of Architecture by Douglas Murphy was released in late December. The book explores the alternative architectural futures envisioned by designers such as Fuller. Click for further details.

ShararaTech Challenge

The ShararaTech Challenge Call for Submissions on Water, Energy and Self-Reliance around the Mediterranean and its deserts is open for submissions until March 31st, 2016.

The Call for Proposals seeks desert artworks that represent innovative water/energy and self reliance solutions for local communities on or around the Mediterranean.

SOCAP Interviews Bren Smith of GreenWave

Early this month, SOCAP released an in-depth interview with Bren Smith, founder of GreenWave, the aquaculture model that won the 2015 Fuller Challenge.

Living Breakwaters Featured on Smithsonian.com

In an article titled "Oysters Could Save Staten Island From the Next Hurricane Sandy", 2014 Fuller Challenge Winner Living Breakwaters is featured as a unique strategy that integrates storm protection and environmental restoration.

Ecovative Cited in New Plastics Economy Report

2013 Fuller Challenge Winner Ecovative was recently cited in a report by the World Economic Forum and Ellen MacArthur Foundation, with analytical support from McKinsey & Company.

Eleven Competition: Architectural Solutions for San Francisco's Tenderloin

Following on from the success of the Cambodia 2015 competition, BFI Outreach Partner Eleven is excited to announce their latest architecture and idea challenge: ‘San Francisco 2016 - Tenderloin System Update’. Click for details from the press release.

Impact Hub Global Seeks Managing Director

BFI Outreach Partner Impact Hub Global is seeking a Managing Director for Global Membership Experience Development.

"R. Buckminster Fuller: The History (And Mystery) of the Universe " in San Diego

"R. BUCKMINSTER FULLER: THE HISTORY (AND MYSTERY) OF THE UNIVERSE" returns to the Lyceum Stage in San Diego, California from March 10-April 3. BFI Members can claim complimentary tickets to a preview performance preceded by a members-only event. Click for details.

"Inventing Fuller" Exhibition at the University of Arkansas

"Inventing Fuller," an exhibition exploring the work of Fuller as "inventor" through models, drawings, and sketches, is on display through March 18 in the Fred and Mary Smith Exhibition Gallery, in Vol Walker Hall, on the University of Arkansas campus in Fayetteville. It is presented by the Fay Jones School of Architecture and Design.

BFI and Kurt Przybilla Present: Explorations in the Geometry of Thinking

Join BFI and Kurt Przybilla at the Hammer Museum at UCLA in Los Angeles on March 10th at 7:30pm for a free workshop entitled "Explorations in the Geometry of Thinking".
